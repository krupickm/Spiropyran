---

Write a concise session summary suitable for a commit message or `NOTES.md` entry.

Cover:
1. **What was done** — files changed, behaviours added or modified.
2. **Why** — one or two sentences linking back to the spec or to a chemistry-level reason.
3. **What was decided** — any non-obvious design choices made during the session, with the reasoning. This is the most valuable part for future-you.
4. **What was deferred** — things noticed but not addressed, with rough notes on follow-up.
5. **Test status** — pass/fail counts.
6. **Spec impact** — whether `project.md` was updated, or should be.

Format as plain Markdown. No emojis. No marketing language. Aim for under 30 lines.

Do not commit. Just write the summary; the user decides where it goes.
